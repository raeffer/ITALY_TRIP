# **Day 1 --- Ravenna**

**Theme:** *The mosaics that stopped an empire.*

## **Overview**

**Driving distance:** ≈85 km from San Matteo della Decima (direct route)

**Driving time:** ≈1h15 (direct)

**General notes:** Compact, walkable historic centre in Ravenna --- park
once, do everything on foot. 13 September 2026 is a Sunday --- several
Ravenna businesses below have reduced Sunday hours, noted individually.

**Routing note:** The direct route (via Cento, Sant\'Agostino, Argenta)
crosses flat agricultural plain --- nothing along it earns a stop. Real
alternative worth considering: route via Comacchio instead --- adds
roughly 45--60 minutes each way, but trades the empty plain for a
genuinely different, atmospheric detour: pastel canal houses (\"Piccola
Venezia\"), and the Manifattura dei Marinati, a historic eel-curing and
smoking operation tied to centuries of Comacchio lagoon fishing. The
Valli di Comacchio lagoon (pink flamingos) and the 9th-century Abbazia
di Pomposa are further optional add-ons. This turns Day 1 from an easy
transit day into a longer, more active one --- a genuine choice, not a
free upgrade.

## **Stops**

## **Coffee**

**Caffè Il Nazionale** --- Piazza del Popolo 28

*📍 44.4175944, 12.1995888 📞 +39 0544 217668 · Hours: 7:00am--11:00pm
daily*

Why: historic café on Ravenna\'s main square, part of the city\'s civic
memory since the early 1900s. Current owner Franco Scarfati trained in
Campania\'s top pastry houses before taking over in 2016, and won the
national \"Top Quality\" silver award at Italy\'s Migliori Dolci
d\'Italia in 2019.

*Honest flag: Google shows this at 3.1★/609 reviews --- a real pattern
of complaints about slow, dismissive, or rude counter service, not just
a minor gripe. The pastry pedigree is genuine; the service experience is
a real coin-flip. Order at the bar, and don\'t be surprised if it\'s
brusque.*

*Checked for a better alternative on this pass: Grinder Coffee Lab (Via
di Roma 178) is a genuine third-wave specialty coffee lab with
consistently strong reviews ("Starbucks go home, three times better, a
third of the price") --- a better cup than Il Nazionale by a clear
margin. But it's closed Sundays, and 13 September 2026 (the actual
travel day) is a Sunday, so it doesn't work for this trip. Also
investigated Bar Byron, a historic 1951 café named for Lord Byron (who
lived in Ravenna) and once a hangout for writers and cyclists Bartali
and Coppi --- but it closed in 2023 and the space is now a restaurant
called Clarice, so it's not a valid pick. Il Nazionale remains the
practical choice for this specific date despite the service flag above.*

## **Pasticceria**

**Pasticceria Veneziana** --- Via Salara 15

*📍 44.4194468, 12.1982703 📞 +39 0544 212171 · Closed Mondays;

Founded 1864 by a Venetian family, run by the Baccarini family since
1964 (took over on its 100th anniversary). Gambero Rosso--listed,

two families, not a rebrand.

## **What to Buy**

## **Hidden Gem**

**Mercato Coperto** --- Piazza Andrea Costa 6

*📍 44.418912, 12.199129 📞 +39 0544 244611 · Open daily
9:00am--midnight*

A genuinely historic site --- origins trace to medieval fish and drapery
markets at the confluence of two now-vanished rivers; the current 1918
building has exposed brick and Istrian marble, officially recognised in
2008 as a site of historic-artistic interest, restored through 2019.
Dedicated salumeria, pescheria, and a fresh-pasta stall where sfogline
hand-roll cappelletti daily.

*Caution from a reviewer: \"we expected lots of different stalls ---
instead there\'s a huge bar, then more bars, and a wine shop.\" Manage
expectations; graze rather than plan a full sit-down meal.*

#  **Day 2 --- San Marino & Fano**

**Theme:** *A whole other country before lunch.*

## **Overview**

**Driving distance:** ≈150 km including the San Marino detour

**Driving time:** ≈2h30

**General notes:** 14 September 2026 is a Monday. Most places below
operate normally on Mondays, but Trattoria La Quinta (Fano) is closed
Mondays --- flagged below, swapped out of the picks accordingly.

**Routing note (Ravenna → San Marino):** No noteworthy stops on this leg
--- motorway and open plain.

**Routing note (San Marino → Fano):** Take the SP44 up to Gabicce Monte
and through Parco Naturale Monte San Bartolo rather than the default
motorway route --- a genuinely panoramic clifftop road. Honest caveat
from a recent reviewer: \"you won\'t see anything unless you stop at the
designated viewpoints\... parking places were full.\" Treat it as a
scenic detour with a handful of good pull-offs, not a continuously
scenic drive --- and don\'t expect to park at every viewpoint in peak
season.

## **Stops**

### **San Marino --- Guaita Tower & Monte Titano**

*📍 43.9354691, 12.4493514 --- Salita Alla Rocca, 47890 Città di San
Marino 📞 +378 0549 991369*

-   **Why stop:** The oldest and most iconic of San Marino\'s Three
    Towers, 11th century, perched on the summit of Monte Titano --- the
    single best view over the republic and the Adriatic plain below.

-   **Time required:** 2--3 hours for the towers, Piazza della Libertà,
    and a wander

-   **Worth the detour:** Yes --- a genuinely independent microstate,
    not a theme-park version of one

-   **Parking:** Paid lots below the historic centre; it\'s a walk
    uphill from most of them

-   **Approx cost:** €11 combined ticket covers Guaita, Cesta Tower, and
    museums

-   **Hours:** 8:30am--7:45pm daily

### **Piazza della Libertà & Palazzo Pubblico**

*📍 43.9367403, 12.4465224 --- Contrada del Pianello, 47890 Città di San
Marino 📞 +378 0549 883152*

-   **Why stop:** The seat of San Marino\'s government --- Captains
    Regent elected every six months, one of the world\'s oldest
    continuous republican systems. Neo-Gothic, built 1884--94.

-   **Time required:** 20--30 min (exterior; often closed to the public
    inside)

### **Fano --- Arco di Augusto**

*📍 43.8430963, 13.0145177 --- Via Arco D\'Augusto, 61032 Fano PU*

-   **Why stop:** A well-preserved Roman-era arch marking the entrance

-   **Time required:** 20--30 min

-   **Worth the detour:** Yes --- it\'s the entrance to the old town

**La Serenissima** --- Via Venticinque Marzo 67 (the actual Torta Tre

*📍 43.9531511, 12.4685640 📞 +378 0549 878102 · Mon--Fri

San Marino\'s national cake, made by the same family operation since

## **San Marino --- Cool, Interesting & Quirky**

**Cava dei Balestrieri** --- Via Eugippo

*📍 43.9375482, 12.4457523 · 4.7★/700 reviews*

The training ground of San Marino\'s Crossbowmen --- a corps with
records dating to the 14th century, originally defending the city walls,
now a living folk tradition with public exhibitions and a small museum
of costumes and trophies. One reviewer happened on a live crossbow shoot
with spectators --- worth checking the Federation\'s calendar if timing
allows, but atmospheric even empty.

## **Fano --- Coffee**

**Caffè Cavour** --- Via Cavour 1

*📍 43.8400612, 13.0195388 · Closed Sundays; Mon 5:30am--8:00pm*

## **Local Speciality**

San Marino: piadina sammarinese, cappelletti with fresh egg pasta,
Sangiovese and Brugneto (reds), Biancale (white).

Fano: Brodetto alla fanese (the local Adriatic fish stew). Moretta
fanese --- the sailors\' coffee-and-liqueur digestivo (coffee, rum,
brandy, anise, lemon peel), a genuine local institution, not a tourist
gimmick --- first documented 1908, entered AIBES\'s official cocktail
list in 2006.

## **What to Buy**

San Marino: Torta Tre Monti from La Serenissima (see above). Fano: a
bottle of something to make Moretta at home, or Sacher torte from
Longhini.

## **Hidden Gem**

Caffè Cavour, Fano --- see Coffee above. The genuine paper-trail
birthplace of Moretta fanese is a stronger hidden-gem story than
anything else found for Fano this pass --- most other options were
directory listings without a real story attached.

*A note on Fano\'s boatbuilding history: the port had three successive
squeri (traditional wooden boatyards) along the port canal, the last one
still recorded as an active launching slip as of 2012. Chased this down
properly and the honest result is that no currently-active, visitable,
family-run wooden boatbuilder survives in Fano itself --- the trade
seems to have folded into modern fibreglass/steel yards. The region\'s
real surviving wooden-boat specialists are further down the coast:
Cantiere Foschi and Cantiere Boschetti in Cesenatico, Cantiere Navale
Gori in Rimini. What does survive in Fano: the street names Via dello
Squero and Piazzale dei Calafati (\"Caulkers\' Square\"), right by the
old port --- literal fossils of the trade, worth a passing look rather
than a dedicated stop.*

#  **Day 3 --- Sirolo (Conero Riviera)**

**Theme:** *Where the mountain meets the sea, and the mussels are
hand-picked by divers.*

## **Overview**

**Driving distance:** ≈95 km from Fano

**Driving time:** ≈1h30, more with stops

**General notes:** 15 September 2026 is a Tuesday. Most picks below
operate normally; exceptions flagged individually (Gelateria del Conero
closed Mondays --- not relevant; Diecidodici restaurant closed Mondays
--- not relevant; Abbazia di Portonovo only opens Thu/Sat 5--7pm ---
relevant, see Portonovo note).

**Routing note:** Senigallia sits directly on the coastal route south
from Fano, roughly 30km down --- worth a real stop, not just a beach
town. The Rocca Roveresca (a well-preserved 15th-century fortress, €5
entry) and the historic centre justify 45--60 minutes. Continue south to
Portonovo before Sirolo.

## **Stops**

### **Senigallia --- Rocca Roveresca**

*📍 43.7153332, 13.2204474 --- Piazza del Duca, 2, 60019 Senigallia AN
📞 +39 071 63258*

-   **Why stop:** A genuinely well-preserved 15th-century defensive
    fortress right by the train station, with an elaborately restored
    interior and views over the old town. More substance than the
    \"Velvet Beach\" reputation suggests.

-   **Time required:** 45--60 min

-   **Approx cost:** €5 entry

-   **Hours:** 8:30am--7:30pm daily

### **Portonovo --- Abbazia di Santa Maria di Portonovo**

*📍 43.5611908, 13.5999370 --- 60020 Portonovo AN*

-   **Why stop:** An 11th-century Romanesque abbey church right on the
    beach --- genuinely rare architecture; reviewers specifically note
    only Corsica and Normandy have comparable examples of this style.
    Ruins of a Benedictine abbey surround it.

-   **Time required:** 30--60 min, or longer for a swim at Portonovo\'s
    bay

-   **Hours --- important:** Only open Thursday and Saturday,
    5:00--7:00pm. If your visit doesn\'t land on one of those
    days/times, you\'ll see it from outside only --- still worth the
    stop for the setting, but plan around this if going inside matters
    to you.

### **Sirolo --- the Conero Riviera\'s prettiest base**

*📍 43.5196105, 13.6180163 --- Via Peschiera 30, 60020 Sirolo AN (Parco
del Conero office) 📞 +39 071 933 1161*

-   **Why stop:** Monte Conero is the rare spot on this coast where a
    mountain drops straight into the Adriatic. Sirolo itself is a
    restored medieval hill town with a small piazza looking straight
    down onto the sea.

-   **Time required:** Full day, ideally with an overnight

-   **Worth the detour:** Yes --- the best swimming of the whole
    northern run

-   **Parking:** Paid lots near Spiaggia Urbani fill up fast in season;
    other Conero beaches are shuttle- or foot-access only

## **Coffee**

## **Lunch**

## **Local Speciality**

Moscioli --- wild mussels hand-harvested by free-diving fishermen
(mosciolari) from the Cooperativa Pescatori di Portonovo, not farmed,
only between Portonovo and Sirolo, only May--September. A Slow Food
Presidio product since 2004. Spaghetti coi moscioli, moscioli alla
marinara, or impepata di moscioli are the classic preparations. Real
caveat: the population has taken a serious hit from marine heatwaves in
recent years --- see the Quirky section below before assuming they\'ll
be plentiful or cheap on your date.

## **Cool, Interesting & Quirky**

**Cooperativa Pescatori di Portonovo** --- the real, named cooperative
behind the moscioli tradition

Founded on the last professional mosciolo divers working the Conero
coast --- named fishermen like Massimo Manganelli, Massimiliano
Stecconi, and Sandro Rocchetti free-dive 5--10 metres to pull moscioli
off the rocks by hand using a moscioliniera, a tool evolved from the
pitchfork farmers originally used when this started in the early 1900s.
You can buy directly from them --- either at their beach capanni in
Portonovo itself when they\'re in, or at their fixed sales points in
Numana and Osimo, where they also sell other Adriatic fish and
ready-to-eat seafood. This is the real version of \"meet the people who
do this\" rather than a tour built around the tradition.

*Timing honesty: Mosciolando, the annual festival dedicated to the
mosciolo (Mosciolino d\'Oro award, a boat race with traditional batane,
beachside tastings), is confirmed for 18--21 June 2026 --- well before
your September window. Worth knowing it exists for a future trip, but it
won\'t be running when you\'re there.*

*Current status, worth knowing before you go: 2024 reporting (Il Resto
del Carlino, and ongoing research from Università Politecnica delle
Marche) documented a serious die-off from marine heatwaves --- \"nothing
left near shore\" in some studied areas as of late 2024. The cooperative
and Slow Food are actively managing the fishery to protect what\'s left.
Don\'t assume abundant, cheap moscioli in September 2026 --- ask the
cooperative or your restaurant directly what the season actually looks
like this year.*

#  **Day 4 --- Ortona & San Vito Chietino (Costa dei Trabocchi)**

**Theme:** *Dinner suspended over the sea on a working fishing machine.*

## **Overview**

**Driving distance:** ≈210 km from Sirolo, including the Ortona stop

**Driving time:** ≈3h15

**General notes:** 16 September 2026 is a Wednesday. This is genuinely
the emptiest, longest driving day on the route --- most of it is

deliberately rather than rushing.

**Routing note --- Loreto (optional):** The Basilica della Santa Casa is
barely off the direct route and is one of the most significant Marian
pilgrimage sites in Christianity (alongside Lourdes and Fatima) --- not
\"just another church.\" Free entry, open 6:30am--7:30pm daily. Whether
it\'s worth the stop depends entirely on your interest level; flagging
it as a genuine option rather than a must, given the brief\'s general
steer away from church-hopping. Even setting the basilica aside,
Pasticceria Picchio right at its base is worth the detour on its own ---
see below.

*📍 43.4410473, 13.6102970 --- Piazza della Madonna, 1, 60025 Loreto AN
📞 +39 071 974 7155*

-   **Why stop:** Houses what tradition holds to be the actual home of

### **Pasticceria Picchio --- right at the base of Loreto**

*📍 43.4466207, 13.6175661 --- Via Traversa E. Rampolla 2, 60025 Loreto
Stazione AN 📞 +39 071 977760 · Closed Mondays; Tue--Sun 6:30am--10pm ·
4.6★/2,236 reviews*

This is genuinely one of the most awarded pastry shops in Italy, not
just a local favourite. Founded in 1978 by Elisabetta Marcozzi, now
under maestro pasticciere Claudio Marcozzi, Il Picchio has held Gambero
Rosso\'s top rating --- \"Tre Tazzine & Tre Chicchi\" --- for ten

pizza/restaurant service at lunch and dinner if timing works out. If
you\'re doing the Loreto detour at all, this alone is worth building the
stop around.

### **San Vito Chietino --- the heart of the Costa dei Trabocchi**

*📍 42.2963540, 14.4436070 --- San Vito Chietino, Chieti*

-   **Why stop:** A trabocco is a wooden fishing platform built on
    stilts over the water, allowing fishermen to work without boats ---
    genuinely distinctive engineering, not replicated elsewhere on this
    route. The most famous, Trabocco Turchino, was immortalised by
    Gabriele D\'Annunzio in Il Trionfo della Morte --- named either for
    the nearby Capo Turchino headland or, more romantically, for a local
    fisherman\'s sun-darkened skin.

-   **Time required:** Half day plus dinner

-   **Worth the detour:** Yes --- genuinely distinctive, not replicated
    anywhere else on this route

-   **Why stop:** The headland associated with Gabriele D\'Annunzio\'s

#  **Day 7 --- Vieste (Gargano)**

**Theme:** *A pastry shop named after a massacre memorial, and a beach
ruled by a heartbroken stone giant.*

## **Overview**

**Driving distance:** ≈110 km from Termoli

**Driving time:** ≈2--2.5 hours via the Gargano coast road

**General notes:** 19 September 2026 is a Saturday.

**Routing note:** The SS16 bridge over the Trigno (flagged on Day 6) may
still force the A14 diversion for the start of this leg --- recheck
closer to the date. Optional detour: Lago di Lesina, a lagoon with a
genuine eel-fishing tradition (capitoni), wooden fishermen\'s huts on
stilts, and a small naturalistic museum. Sits somewhat off the most
direct route rather than squarely on it --- a real choice, not a given,
similar to the Comacchio call on Day 1.

## **Stops**

### **Vieste centro storico & Castello Svevo**

*📍 41.882, 16.176 --- Vieste FG*

-   **Why stop:** A 13th-century fortress with sweeping Adriatic views,
    anchoring one of the Gargano\'s prettiest old towns --- white stone
    alleys, a Puglian-Romanesque cathedral, and a genuine working port
    below.

-   **Time required:** 1.5--2 hours

### **Spiaggia di Castello & Pizzomunno**

*📍 41.8673500, 16.1753703 --- Spiaggia di Castello, 71019 Vieste FG*

-   **Why stop:** A 25-metre limestone monolith on the beach, with its
    own local legend --- a fisherman named Pizzomunno turned to stone by
    grief after mermaids stole his love and dragged her into the sea.

-   **Time required:** 1--2 hours, or a full beach afternoon

-   **Practical note:** Gets crowded and the water gets murkier near the
    crowds at peak times --- locals suggest walking \~200m further along
    the beach for cleaner water and fewer people.

### **Chianca Amara**

*📍 41.8815296, 16.1814304 --- Via Gregorio XIII, 71019 Vieste FG*

-   **Why stop:** A small, easy-to-miss stone memorial to a genuinely
    dark piece of local history --- in 1554, the corsair Dragut Rais\'s
    forces besieged Vieste for seven days and executed the elderly,
    women, and children who couldn\'t be sold as slaves on this exact
    rock. 4.5★/24 reviews. One reviewer notes it\'s so unobtrusively
    placed that \"nessuno guarda\" --- nobody really looks --- worth
    deliberately seeking out rather than stumbling on.

-   **Time required:** 10--15 min

## **Coffee & Pasticceria**

**Cornetteria Chianca Amara** --- Via Nicolò Cimaglia 4

*📍 41.8814613, 16.1815773 📞 +39 389 528 4921 · 4.4★/75 reviews*

Took its name directly from the memorial stone next door --- a genuinely
nice pairing of history and food, right in the old town. Reviewers
specifically single out the coda d\'aragosta (\"lobster tail\" pastry,
crisp choux filled with cream) and the pistachio cornetti --- \"the best
we\'ve ever had,\" per one review. Hours run into the evening
(6pm--midnight most days) as much as the morning, so it works as an
after-dinner stop too.

**Alternate:** Bakery Café Sant\'Antonio --- Piazzale Aldo Moro 64

*📍 41.8842286, 16.1696023 📞 +39 0884 706484 · 4.4★/351 reviews, open
5:00am--9:00pm daily*

42+ years running, recently refreshed in style without losing the
substance --- reviewers repeatedly call out specific staff by name
(Stefania, Stella) for warmth and consistency, and it\'s frequently
cited as \"best coffee in Vieste.\"

## **Dinner**

**Donlù** --- Largo Seggio 8

*📞 +39 0884 592264 · 4.6★/93 reviews*

Family-run --- Donato and Luca are named specifically by reviewers, and
the small rustic dining room with domed stone roofs keeps things
intimate rather than polished. Creative Apulian dishes rather than
straight tradition: the scallop-and-urchin starter, the octopus dish,
and the tiramisu are the most consistently praised. One dissenting
review calls it an "overhyped tourist trap" alongside a majority of
strong ratings --- worth knowing going in that reactions aren't
unanimous.

**Alternate:** Osteria degli Archi --- Via Ripe 2

*📞 +39 0884 705199 · 4.3--4.5★ across 1,400+ reviews*

Chef/patron Michele Vescera's restaurant is cut directly into the rock
cliff of the Ripa, looking out over Pizzomunno beach --- stone arches
and wooden beams inside. Repeatedly called "maybe the best restaurant in
Vieste" in reviews. Noticeably pricier than most Vieste alternatives
(€40--50/person) --- the tradeoff for the quality and the view.

**Budget alternate:** Box 19 --- Via Santa Maria di Merino 13

*📞 +39 0884 705229 · 3.6--4.3★ across 1,200+ reviews*

The genuine budget pick of the three --- casual seafood, and reviewers
specifically mention locals recommending it for lunch. Honest read on
the mixed reviews: some call it the best-value fish in town, others
describe brusque or dismissive service on off nights. Go in with modest
expectations on service rather than food.

## **Local Speciality**

Paposcia --- a Vieste-specific baked stuffed bread (mozzarella, tomato,
rocket, or whatever\'s local that day). \'U ciambott --- the local fish
soup. Orecchiette with cime di rapa. Alici nere di Vieste --- a distinct
dark anchovy variety specific to this stretch of coast.

## **What to Buy**

Extra virgin olive oil --- the Gargano\'s Ogliarola Garganica olives
feed the Dauno DOP oil, and small producers sell directly from roadside
stands between Vieste and Mattinata (worth a stop if you see one, rather
than seeking out a specific address). Vieste\'s Monday morning market
runs along the lungomare from early morning to lunchtime --- olive oil,
taralli, cheeses, fresh fruit, and handmade goods including regional
decorated whistles (fischietti) --- genuine local handicraft, though the
deeper home of that specific craft is further south near Bari, worth
knowing rather than assuming Vieste invented it.

## **Hidden Gem**

Chianca Amara and its neighbouring pastry shop together --- see Stops
and Coffee above. The combination of a quiet, easy-to-overlook 1554
massacre memorial next to a beloved pastry shop that took its name and
identity directly from that stone is exactly the kind of pairing that
rewards knowing to look for it.

## **Cool, Interesting & Quirky**

The Pizzomunno legend itself is worth telling properly on the beach, not
just noting as a landmark: a young fisherman in love with a mermaid,
stolen away by jealous rival mermaids who couldn\'t understand mortal
love, turned to stone by grief when he couldn\'t reach her. Locals will
tell you the sea still \"calls\" to him. A nice piece of oral folklore
to have in your pocket while you\'re actually looking at the rock.

## **If Time Is Tight**

Skip Chianca Amara and the market. Walk the centro storico, see
Pizzomunno, eat at Cornetteria Chianca Amara for breakfast regardless
--- it\'s on the way through the old town anyway.

## **If You\'ve Got Half a Day**

Add Chianca Amara itself, a proper swim past the crowded stretch of
Spiaggia di Castello, and if it\'s a Monday, the lungomare market.

## **Skip It**

Don\'t force the Lesina detour if time\'s tight --- it\'s genuinely
interesting but adds real driving time for a lagoon-and-eels experience
that\'s a specific taste, not a broad crowd-pleaser.

## **Stops**

### **Baia delle Zagare**

*📍 41.7311, 16.0857 --- Comune di Mattinata, FG (approximate --- cove
itself has no street address)*

-   **Why stop:** Two limestone faraglioni rising from turquoise water
    between white cliffs --- one of the Gargano\'s most photographed
    spots, and genuinely earns it.

-   **Time required:** Half day if you get in; the access process alone
    can eat an hour

-   **Access --- read this before planning around it:** This is
    genuinely complicated and worth knowing in advance rather than
    discovering on arrival. Free public beach access is capped at 20
    passes/day in high season, issued each morning at the Info Point,
    Corso Matino 64, Mattinata (arrive early --- queues form before
    opening; all adults in the party must be present with ID; a pass
    can\'t be requested again within 6 days of a previous one).
    Alternatives: a paid cliff elevator down to the beach, day-use
    sunbed hire at the Baia dei Mergoli lido, or free access if staying
    at the on-site hotel. Reported costs online vary wildly, from
    roughly €15 for access up to sunbed/parking fees reported near €70+
    combined --- treat any specific figure as unverified until you check
    locally. One genuine piece of good news: 20 September is past the
    deepest of high season, so the 20-pass cap and queues may be
    substantially eased or lifted by your date --- worth calling the
    Info Point (+39 0884 597291) ahead rather than assuming August-level
    restrictions.

-   **Practical note:** The bay falls into shade by mid-afternoon due to
    the surrounding cliffs --- go in the morning if the sun matters to

#  **Day 9 --- Monte Sant\'Angelo & Trani**

## **Overview**

*📍 41.7080134, 15.9547699 --- Via Reale Basilica, 127, 71037 Monte

### **Option B: Bari**

Bigger, livelier, with Bari Vecchia\'s old town --- including Strada

**Dinner --- Best pick:** Al Pescatore, Piazza Federico II di Svevia 6/8

\$\$\$ --- 50+ year seafood institution by Castello Svevo in Bari

**Alternate:** Al Sorso Preferito / Urban Assassineria --- the Spaghetti

**Budget pick:** Panificio Fiore, Bari Vecchia --- the historic

genuine significance across both Catholic and Orthodox Christianity.
Worth a look if walking through the old town regardless of the
accommodation choice.

**Choosing between them:** Molfetta if tonight should be quiet and
simple after a long day. Bari if there\'s appetite for one more proper
city --- bigger, louder, with more to see, at the cost of a less restful
night before tomorrow\'s final push to Cisternino.

## **Cool, Interesting & Quirky**

**Strada Arco Basso, Bari Vecchia** --- relevant if choosing Bari
tonight, or worth a detour even if sleeping in Molfetta

*📍 41.1273126, 16.8671134 --- Arco Basso, 70122 Bari BA*

Bari Vecchia\'s famous \"orecchiette street\" --- women working at
floured tables outside their own front doors, hand-shaping orecchiette
with a quick thumb motion, pasta laid out to dry on frames overhead.
Genuinely famous: locals like Nunzia have represented Bari at
international tourism events including a New York Times travel show, and
the scene has appeared in a Dolce & Gabbana campaign.

Important honesty flag, found via a credible local source
(discoverbari.it): some reporting alleges that a portion of what\'s sold
here as hand-made on the spot is actually wholesale pasta from factories
in the Bari province, bought in bulk and resold at artisanal prices ---
with the visible hand-shaping serving partly as a performance for
tourists and photos rather than the literal source of every bag sold.
This doesn\'t mean the craft itself is fake --- the skill on display is
completely real and has been passed down for generations, as the
women\'s own accounts confirm --- but it does mean the honest way to
experience this is to stop and genuinely watch the technique (the speed
is the part worth noticing, more than a quick photo), rather than
assuming every purchase is a small-batch product from the exact hands
you\'re watching.

## **If Time Is Tight**

Skip Monte Sant\'Angelo\'s town wander and stick to the Basilica itself.
In Trani, the cathedral exterior and a walk along the port cover the
essentials.

## **If You\'ve Got Half a Day**

Climb Trani\'s cathedral tower, and set aside 20--30 minutes to actually
watch (not just photograph) the orecchiette-making on Strada Arco Basso
if the Bari route is in play.

## **Skip It**

Don\'t buy orecchiette on Strada Arco Basso specifically expecting
small-batch artisanal sourcing given the sourcing question above --- buy
it as a souvenir and a way of supporting the street\'s continued life,
not as a guarantee of provenance.

## **Today\'s Winners**

-   **🏆 Best coffee/pastry:** Bar Europa, Trani --- for the Tette delle
    Monache

-   **🏆 Best lunch:** Ristorante Medioevo, Monte Sant\'Angelo

-   **🏆 Best experience:** The Santuario di San Michele Arcangelo

-   **🏆 Best purchase:** A photograph and a genuine 10 minutes of
    attention on Strada Arco Basso, more than the pasta itself

#  **Day 10 --- Polignano a Mare, Monopoli & On to San Vito dei Normanni**

## **Stops**

### **Polignano a Mare --- Lama Monachile**

*📍 40.9963887, 17.2181307 --- Via S. Vito, 70044 Polignano a Mare BA*

🅿️ **🟡 Can be busy** --- free parking in white-marked areas only (yellow-blocked lines are residents-only); arrive by 9:00am for a decent spot in season

⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️ Worth changing today's plans for

-   **Why stop:** The postcard cove between two cliffs, houses stacked
    right to the water's edge above it --- genuinely as striking in
    person as in photos, though also genuinely crowded.

-   **Time required:** 1--2 hours

-   **Practical note:** Not a sandy beach --- bring water shoes for the
    large pebbles.

### **Monopoli --- centro storico**

*📍 40.9508368, 17.3033327 --- Largo Cattedrale, 70043 Monopoli BA 📞
+39 080 742253*

🅿️ **🟡 Can be busy** --- closest paid option is Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II (€1/h, €8/day cap in summer, 118 spaces); free white-line spots at Piazza XX Settembre or Via Procaccia if you're happy to walk further

⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️ Worth a real detour

-   **Why stop:** Genuinely less touristed than Polignano or Bari
    despite being just as handsome --- the Cattedrale della Madia (free
    entry, marble-clad interior notably different from the plainer
    churches in Bari and Trani), Castello Carlo V on its own promontory,
    and a working old port with fishing boats rather than a performance
    of one.

-   **Time required:** 1.5--2 hours

-   **Worth the detour:** Yes --- described repeatedly as "authentic
    Puglia, not yet touched by mass tourism" relative to its more
    famous neighbours

-   **Hours (Castello):** 10:00am--2:00pm and 4:00--8:00pm daily

### **Ostuni --- centro storico ("La Terra")**

*📍 40.7333725, 17.5778841 --- Centro Storico, 72017 Ostuni BR*

🅿️ **🟡 Can be busy** --- the historic centre itself is ZTL (camera-enforced, no through traffic); park just outside the walls at Via Tenente Specchia or Foro Boario (€4--8/day flat rate) and walk in 5--10 minutes

⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️ Worth changing today's plans for

-   **Why stop:** The famous "White City" --- a hill town of whitewashed
    houses and narrow stepped alleys inside Aragonese walls, plus the
    Gothic Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta with its rose window. Sits
    almost exactly between Monopoli and San Vito dei Normanni.

-   **Time required:** Afternoon through early evening, including
    Riccardo Caffè below

-   **Best time:** Late afternoon into early evening --- Piazzetta
    Martiri delle Foibe (just outside the old walls) gives the classic
    postcard view of the town on its hill.

-   **Honest note:** In peak season the centre gets busy with
    day-trippers by early afternoon --- the walk between the cathedral
    and the walls has genuinely quiet backstreets even when the main
    routes are crowded.

## **Coffee (Polignano a Mare)**

**Caffè speciale** --- the genuine local curiosity, not a café by name
but a drink: espresso whipped together with sugar, lemon peel, cream,
and amaretto liqueur. Available at most bars around the old town and
Lama Monachile --- ask for it by name rather than expecting it on a
menu everywhere. This is the actual "why" here, rather than chasing a
best-espresso pick that doesn't really exist as a distinct category in
Polignano.

## **Pasticceria (Ostuni)**

**Forno 31** --- Via G. Ferrari, 6, 72017 Ostuni BR

A working bakery built around an original 15th-century wood-fired
oven --- reportedly the largest the reviewers who found it had ever
seen, worked with 2-metre-long oven paddles. Makes pasticciotti in
multiple fillings (custard, chocolate, sour cherry, pistachio), plus
focaccia, taralli, and friselle. A genuine "found us by accident,
bought everything left" kind of place rather than a tourist-trail
stop.

## **Lunch (Monopoli)**

**Madià**, at the Porto Antico --- panzerotto, eaten on a bench by the
old port watching the fishing boats. A genuine local street-food
ritual rather than a sit-down meal, which suits the day's pacing.

**Alternate:** Locanda dei Mercanti, reportedly the best pasticciotto
in town (served as a "tris" of three fillings), also does savoury
lunch if you want to sit down properly.

## **Dinner (San Vito dei Normanni --- arrival night)**

**XFood** --- Via Mare snc, inside the ExFadda cultural centre, 72019
San Vito dei Normanni BR 📞 +39 366 891 3041

Puglia's first "social restaurant" --- a project ten years running,
staffed partly by people with disabilities, serving genuinely
well-regarded Pugliese cooking (featured on Gambero Rosso, Slow Food,
and the Touring Club guide, 4.5--4.6★ across 1,100+ reviews). €27--70pp
depending on menu. **Honest flag:** sources conflict on Tuesday opening
hours --- book ahead by phone rather than showing up on spec, since
tonight is a Tuesday.

## **Local Speciality**

Panzerotto --- Puglia's fried, folded street food, best today in
Monopoli. Pasticciotto --- best today at Forno 31 in Ostuni. Caffè
speciale --- Polignano's own coffee-and-amaretto ritual, unique to the
town.

## **What to Buy**

Taralli or friselle from Forno 31 --- unlike the pasticciotto, these
actually travel well and keep for weeks, so they're a genuine
take-home option rather than an eat-immediately one.

## **Hidden Gems**

**Dorsale --- fish charcuterie lab, Polignano a Mare**

A fish butcher that cures seafood the way a salumeria cures pork ---
squid-and-tuna "mortadella," salmon-pistachio prosciutto, swordfish
ham, tuna guanciale. Opened December 2025, the first of its kind.
Tasting stools on-site. A genuine "how did you find this" stop rather
than a meal --- treat it as a curiosity tasting between Lama Monachile
and departure, not a lunch substitute.

**Riccardo Caffè --- rock-carved bar, Ostuni**

*📍 Via Gaetano Tanzarella Vitale 61, 72017 Ostuni BR · Opens 6:00pm
daily*

A former 13th-century flour mill, now a bar carved directly into the
white limestone --- stalactites, stone rooms, and a stepped stone
staircase entrance with a view over the White City. Despite the name,
this is an evening cocktail/aperitivo spot, not a daytime coffee stop
--- it doesn't open until 6:00pm, which is exactly why it's built into
today's schedule as the last Ostuni beat before the final drive.
Genuinely unlike anything else on the route so far.

**Virgola Pasticceria Terapeutica --- San Vito dei Normanni**

*📍 Via Mesagne, 136, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR*

A worthwhile flag for tomorrow morning, since it belongs properly to
the Puglia-basing week rather than tonight: a therapeutic pastry lab
run by Nicola Di Lena (Italy's Best Pastry Chef, Identità Golose
2021), training women who are domestic-violence survivors and young
people on the autism spectrum. Same social project as XFood.

## **Cool, Interesting & Quirky**

Monopoli sits on top of a hidden network of rock-cut Byzantine
churches (rupestrian crypts) carved into the walls of the "lame"
(ravines) outside town --- Cripta dello Spirito Santo and the Chiesa
dei Santi Andrea e Procopio among them, some with surviving medieval
frescoes. **Honest flag:** these are genuinely obscure even by Puglia
standards --- one opens only eight days a year for Easter tradition,
others sit on private masseria land and need a guide arranged in
advance. Not realistic to add to today's route, but worth knowing
about for a future, slower visit to this stretch of coast.

## **Arrival: San Vito dei Normanni**

*📍 40.6563995, 17.7087266 --- Via Principe di Piemonte 13, 72019 San
Vito dei Normanni BR*

Short final hop from Ostuni (~15--20 minutes). This getting-there
document only covers arrival --- San Vito dei Normanni gets its full

Puglia-basing document, since this is the base for the full following
week rather than a single overnight. Virgola and XFood above are early

## **If Time Is Tight**

Skip Monopoli entirely. Polignano (with Dorsale) → Ostuni (with

on its own.

## **If You've Got Half a Day**

Add the full Monopoli old town and castle walk, and take the extra
time at Forno 31 and Ostuni's viewpoint rather than rushing toward
Riccardo Caffè.

## **Skip It**

Don't chase the rupestrian churches today --- they're not accessible
without advance arrangement, and trying anyway will eat time better
spent at Riccardo Caffè.
