Italy Road Trip Companion — Route 1

Day 10
Polignano a Mare, Monopoli & San Vito dei Normanni

"The last coastal day: cove, fish-charcuterie lab, quiet old port, white city, rock-carved aperitivo."
Lama Monachile, Polignano a Mare

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈55-70 km depending on Molfetta/Bari start  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈2-2.5 hours actual driving

22 September 2026 is a Tuesday. This is a full stop-and-go coastal day: Polignano first, Monopoli for the old port and lunch, Ostuni for the late-afternoon White City, then San Vito dei Normanni for the arrival-night base.

Routing note: Polignano comes before Monopoli because it sits further north. That avoids backtracking and gets you to Lama Monachile before the cove gets fully busy.
  • 8:30am: Depart Bari/Molfetta
  • 9:05-10:45am: Lama Monachile, Polignano old town, and caffè speciale
  • ~11:05am: Dorsale fish-charcuterie tasting
  • 11:50am-1:45pm: Monopoli centro storico and lunch
  • 2:25-5:30pm: Ostuni old town, Forno 31, and viewpoint
  • 6:00-6:45pm: Riccardo Caffè aperitivo
  • 7:00pm: Arrive San Vito dei Normanni; 7:30pm dinner at XFood if confirmed
Riccardo Caffè does not open until 6:00pm. Sources disagree on whether XFood is open Tuesdays, so call ahead and book rather than relying on showing up.

Stops

Polignano a Mare — Lama Monachile
📍 40.9963887, 17.2181307 — Via S. Vito, 70044 Polignano a Mare BA  ·  PARKING: can be busy; white-marked areas only
  • Why stop: The postcard cove between two cliffs, with houses stacked to the water's edge above it. It is genuinely striking and genuinely crowded.
  • Time required: 1-2 hours
  • Worth the detour: Worth changing today's plans for
  • Practical note: Not a sandy beach. Bring water shoes for the large pebbles.
Monopoli — Centro Storico
📍 40.9508368, 17.3033327 — Largo Cattedrale, 70043 Monopoli BA  ·  📞 +39 080 742253
Old port, Monopoli
  • Why stop: Less touristed than Polignano or Bari, but just as handsome: Cattedrale della Madia, Castello Carlo V, and a working old port with fishing boats.
  • Time required: 1.5-2 hours
  • Worth the detour: Yes
  • Parking: Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II is the closest paid option; free white-line spots are further out at Piazza XX Settembre or Via Procaccia.
  • Hours (Castello): 10:00am-2:00pm and 4:00-8:00pm daily
Rifugi Antiaerei di Monopoli
Under Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II, Monopoli
  • Why stop: WWII air-raid shelters under the main square, around 300m of tunnels dug into tufo rock, once holding up to 1,800 people during bombing raids. Preserved wartime graffiti and infirmary rooms make it a sharper historical layer under the otherwise sunny old town.
  • Time required: 45–60 min with guided tour
  • Approx cost/hours: Comune-run guided tours, summer weekend hours 18:00–21:00, €3 ticket; book via the Piazza Garibaldi Info Point
Ostuni — Centro Storico ("La Terra")
📍 40.7333725, 17.5778841 — Centro Storico, 72017 Ostuni BR  ·  PARKING: ZTL inside old centre; park outside the walls
Ostuni, the White City
  • Why stop: Whitewashed houses, narrow stepped alleys inside Aragonese walls, and the Gothic Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta with its rose window.
  • Time required: Afternoon through early evening, including Riccardo Caffè
  • Best time: Late afternoon into early evening; Piazzetta Martiri delle Foibe gives the classic town-on-the-hill view.
  • Honest note: In peak season the centre gets busy by early afternoon. The cathedral-to-walls backstreets can still be genuinely quiet.

Coffee & Pasticceria

Caffè speciale Polignano a Mare

The real local curiosity is not a café by name but the drink itself: espresso whipped with sugar, lemon peel, cream, and amaretto liqueur. Ask for it by name around the old town and Lama Monachile.

Forno 31 Ostuni
Via G. Ferrari 6, 72017 Ostuni BR
Pasticciotti with custard and sour cherry

A working bakery built around an original 15th-century wood-fired oven, using 2-metre-long paddles. Go for pasticciotti in multiple fillings, plus focaccia, taralli, and friselle.

Lunch & Dinner

Madià Lunch, Monopoli

At the Porto Antico: panzerotto eaten on a bench by the old port watching fishing boats. This is a local street-food ritual, and it suits the day's pacing better than a long sit-down lunch.

Panzerotti pugliesi
Locanda dei Mercanti Alternate

Reportedly the best pasticciotto in Monopoli, served as a tris of three fillings, with savoury lunch if you want to sit down properly.

XFood Dinner, San Vito dei Normanni
Via Mare snc, inside ExFadda cultural centre, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR  ·  📞 +39 366 891 3041

Puglia's first social restaurant: a ten-year-running project staffed partly by people with disabilities, serving well-regarded Pugliese cooking, with €27-70pp depending on menu.

Honest flag: sources conflict on Tuesday opening hours. Book ahead by phone because tonight is a Tuesday.

Local Speciality

Panzerotto: Puglia's fried, folded street food, best today in Monopoli.

Pasticciotto: best today at Forno 31 in Ostuni.

Caffè speciale: Polignano's own coffee-and-amaretto ritual, unique to the town.

What to Buy

Taralli or friselle from Forno 31. Unlike the pasticciotto, these travel well and keep for weeks, so they are a genuine take-home option rather than an eat-immediately one.

Hidden Gems

Dorsale — fish charcuterie lab, Polignano a Mare

A fish butcher that cures seafood the way a salumeria cures pork: squid-and-tuna mortadella, salmon-pistachio prosciutto, swordfish ham, tuna guanciale. Opened December 2025, the first of its kind. Treat it as a curiosity tasting between Lama Monachile and departure, not a lunch substitute.

Riccardo Caffè — rock-carved bar, Ostuni
📍 Via Gaetano Tanzarella Vitale 61, 72017 Ostuni BR  ·  Opens 6:00pm daily

A former 13th-century flour mill turned evening cocktail/aperitivo bar carved directly into white limestone: stalactites, stone rooms, and a stepped stone entrance with a view over the White City.

Virgola Pasticceria Terapeutica — San Vito dei Normanni
📍 Via Mesagne 136, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR

A tomorrow-morning flag rather than tonight's stop: a therapeutic pastry lab run by Nicola Di Lena, training women who are domestic-violence survivors and young people on the autism spectrum. Same social project as XFood.

Cool, Interesting & Quirky

Monopoli sits on a hidden network of rock-cut Byzantine churches carved into the ravines outside town, including Cripta dello Spirito Santo and Chiesa dei Santi Andrea e Procopio.

Honest flag: these are obscure even by Puglia standards. One opens only eight days a year for Easter tradition; others sit on private masseria land and need a guide arranged in advance. Interesting for a future slower visit, not realistic today.

Arrival: San Vito dei Normanni

📍 40.6563995, 17.7087266 — Via Principe di Piemonte 13, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR

The final hop from Ostuni is about 15-20 minutes. This page only covers arrival; San Vito gets the full local treatment in the separate Puglia-basing document. For tonight: arrive, settle in, dinner at XFood.

If Time Is Tight

Skip Monopoli entirely. Polignano with Dorsale, then Ostuni with Riccardo Caffè, then San Vito dei Normanni is a complete, satisfying day on its own.

If You've Got Half a Day

Add the full Monopoli old town and castle walk, and take the extra time at Forno 31 and Ostuni's viewpoint rather than rushing toward Riccardo Caffè.

Skip It

Do not chase the rupestrian churches today. They are not accessible without advance arrangement, and trying anyway will eat time better spent at Riccardo Caffè.
