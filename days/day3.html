Italy Road Trip Companion — Route 1

Day 3
Sirolo (Conero Riviera)

"Where the mountain meets the sea, and the mussels are hand-picked by divers."
Baia di Portonovo, seen from Monte Conero

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈95 km from Fano  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈1h30, more with stops

15 September 2026 is a Tuesday. Gelateria del Conero closed Mondays (not relevant); Diecidodici restaurant closed Mondays (not relevant); Abbazia di Portonovo only opens Thursday/Saturday 5–7pm (relevant, see note).

Routing note: Senigallia sits directly on the coastal route south from Fano, ~30km down — worth a real stop. The Rocca Roveresca (15th-century fortress, €5 entry) and historic centre justify 45–60 minutes. Continue south to Portonovo before Sirolo.

Stops

Senigallia — Rocca Roveresca
📍 43.7153332, 13.2204474 — Piazza del Duca 2, 60019 Senigallia AN  ·  📞 +39 071 63258
  • Why stop: A well-preserved 15th-century defensive fortress by the train station, elaborately restored interior, views over the old town.
  • Time required: 45–60 min
  • Approx cost: €5 entry
  • Hours: 8:30am–7:30pm daily
Portonovo — Abbazia di Santa Maria di Portonovo
📍 43.5611908, 13.5999370 — 60020 Portonovo AN
Abbazia di Santa Maria di Portonovo
  • Why stop: An 11th-century Romanesque abbey church right on the beach, rare architecture — reviewers note only Corsica and Normandy have comparable examples of this style.
  • Time required: 30–60 min, or longer for a swim
  • Hours — important: Only open Thursday and Saturday, 5:00–7:00pm. Outside those times you'll see it from outside only — still worth the stop for the setting.
Sirolo — the Conero Riviera's prettiest base
📍 43.5196105, 13.6180163 — Via Peschiera 30, 60020 Sirolo AN  ·  📞 +39 071 933 1161
  • Why stop: Monte Conero is the rare spot on this coast where a mountain drops straight into the Adriatic. Sirolo is a restored medieval hill town with a piazza looking straight down onto the sea.
  • Time required: Full day, ideally with an overnight
  • Parking: Paid lots near Spiaggia Urbani fill fast in season

Local Speciality

Moscioli — wild mussels hand-harvested by free-diving fishermen from the Cooperativa Pescatori di Portonovo, only between Portonovo and Sirolo, only May–September. A Slow Food Presidio product since 2004.

Spaghetti with mussels (generic dish photo, not specifically Sirolo moscioli)
Real caveat: population has taken a serious hit from marine heatwaves in recent years — ask if they're actually available before assuming abundance.

Cool, Interesting & Quirky

Cooperativa Pescatori di Portonovo

The real, named cooperative behind the moscioli tradition. Named fishermen (Massimo Manganelli, Massimiliano Stecconi, Sandro Rocchetti) free-dive to harvest by hand using a moscioliniera tool. Buy directly at their Portonovo beach capanni or sales points in Numana/Osimo.

Timing honesty: the Mosciolando festival is 18–21 June 2026 — outside your September window.

If Time Is Tight

Skip Senigallia and Portonovo entirely. Go straight to Sirolo, walk the centro storico, swim at Spiaggia Urbani.

If You've Got Half a Day

Add the Rocca Roveresca in Senigallia on the way through, and the Portonovo abbey (exterior) before Sirolo.

Skip It

Don't force a stop at Gelateria del Conero for food — sea-view drink only.

🏆 Today's Winners

☕ Coffee: Gelateria del Conero (for the view, not the service)
🍝 Lunch: Da Giustina
✨ Experience: Abbazia di Santa Maria di Portonovo
🛍️ Purchase: Moscioli if in season, or Rosso Conero from Bottega dei Sapori Nostrani
