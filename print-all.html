Italy Road Trip Companion — Route 1

Day 1
Ravenna

"The mosaics that stopped an empire."
Mosaic of Theodora, Basilica di San Vitale

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈85 km from San Matteo della Decima  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈1h15 (direct)

Compact, walkable historic centre — park once, do everything on foot. 13 September 2026 is a Sunday — several Ravenna businesses below have reduced Sunday hours, noted individually.

Routing note: the direct route (via Cento, Sant'Agostino, Argenta) crosses flat agricultural plain — nothing along it earns a stop. Real alternative: route via Comacchio instead — adds 45–60 min each way, but trades the plain for pastel canal houses and the Manifattura dei Marinati, a historic eel-curing operation. A genuine choice, not a free upgrade. You do not need to book a tour to see the flamingos. The gated inner Salina di Comacchio (where they nest) is guide-only, but the flamingos themselves feed freely across the open Valli di Comacchio lagoons all day. The best free, public spot is Argine degli Angeli — a walking/cycling path built directly over the lagoon water, starting near Lido di Spina/Stazione di Pesca Bellocchio, with flamingo colonies regularly visible right along the path, sometimes just metres away. Bring binoculars or a long camera lens — their exact position shifts daily depending on feeding. Free public birdwatching towers also exist near Valle Zavelea and along the Via Acciaioli/Lido delle Nazioni road.
Flamingos in the Valli di Comacchio

Stops

Basilica di San Vitale & Mausoleo di Galla Placidia
📍 44.4205557, 12.1963864 / 44.4209827, 12.1971029 — Via San Vitale 17, 48121 Ravenna RA  ·  📞 +39 0544 541688
  • Why stop: The single best mosaic room in Italy — Galla Placidia's 5th-century starry-night ceiling predates San Vitale's 6th-century Byzantine court scenes. Together they anchor Ravenna's eight UNESCO listings. Entry to Galla Placidia is strictly timed (~5 min/person) — expect a short queue.
  • Time required: 1.5–2 hours for both
  • Worth the detour: Yes — non-negotiable
  • Parking: Paid lots ring the centre; walk in, mostly pedestrianised
  • Approx cost: €12.50 cumulative ticket (covers 5 sites)
  • Hours: 9:00am–7:00pm daily (San Vitale), 9:00am–6:30pm daily (Galla Placidia)
Piazza del Popolo & Dante's Tomb
📍 44.4177596, 12.1993185 / 44.4161585, 12.2009368 — Via Dante Alighieri, 48121 Ravenna RA  ·  📞 +39 0544 30297
  • Why stop: The civic heart of the city since the 13th century, plus the grave of a man Florence has spent 700 years trying to get back.
  • Time required: 30–45 min
  • Hours (Tomb): 10:00am–6:00pm daily
Domus dei Tappeti di Pietra
Via Gian Battista Barbiani 16, entrance via Chiesa di Sant'Eufemia, 48121 Ravenna RA
  • Why stop: Underground late-antique residence with over 400 m² of 5th–6th-century polychrome floor mosaics, including the "Danza dei Geni delle Quattro Stagioni." Discovered in 1993 beneath the church and recognised as one of Italy's significant recent archaeological finds.
  • Time required: 30–45 min
  • Worth the detour: Yes — genuinely adjacent to San Vitale, with floor mosaics distinct from Ravenna's apse/wall mosaics.
Museo Byron e del Risorgimento, Palazzo Guiccioli
Via Camillo Benso Cavour 54  ·  Hours: 10:00am–6:00pm, closed Mondays  ·  €10 single ticket
  • Why stop: Reopened November 2024 after major restoration, with 24 rooms across 2,220 m². Byron lived here in 1820–1821, wrote part of Don Juan here, and the palazzo carries a deep Risorgimento/Carbonari thread.
  • Time required: 45–75 min

Coffee

Caffè Il Nazionale Piazza del Popolo 28
📍 44.4175944, 12.1995888  ·  📞 +39 0544 217668  ·  7:00am–11:00pm daily

Historic café on Ravenna's main square, part of the city's civic memory since the early 1900s. Owner Franco Scarfati won the national "Top Quality" silver award at Italy's Migliori Dolci d'Italia in 2019.

Google shows this at 3.1★/609 reviews — a real pattern of complaints about slow, dismissive service. The pastry pedigree is genuine; the service experience is a coin-flip.
Checked for a better alternative on this pass: Grinder Coffee Lab (Via di Roma 178) is a genuine third-wave specialty coffee lab with consistently strong reviews ("Starbucks go home, three times better, a third of the price") — a better cup than Il Nazionale by a clear margin. But it's closed Sundays, and 13 September 2026 (the actual travel day) is a Sunday, so it doesn't work for this trip. Also investigated Bar Byron, a historic 1951 café named for Lord Byron (who lived in Ravenna) and once a hangout for writers and cyclists Bartali and Coppi — but it closed in 2023 and the space is now a restaurant called Clarice. Il Nazionale remains the practical choice for this specific date despite the service flag above.

Hidden Gem

📍 44.418912, 12.199129  ·  📞 +39 0544 244611  ·  Open daily 9am–midnight

Day 2
San Marino & Fano

"A whole other country before lunch."
Guaita Tower on Monte Titano, San Marino

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈150 km including the San Marino detour  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈2h30

14 September 2026 is a Monday.

Routing note (Ravenna → San Marino): No noteworthy stops on this leg — motorway and open plain.
Routing note (San Marino → Fano): Take the SP44 up to Gabicce Monte and through Parco Naturale Monte San Bartolo rather than the default motorway route — a genuinely panoramic clifftop road. Honest caveat: treat it as a scenic detour with good pull-offs, not a continuously scenic drive, and don't expect to park at every viewpoint in peak season.
Gateway to Parco Naturale del Monte San Bartolo, Gabicce

Stops

San Marino — Guaita Tower & Monte Titano
📍 43.9354691, 12.4493514 — Salita Alla Rocca, 47890 Città di San Marino  ·  📞 +378 0549 991369
The Three Towers on the Monte Titano ridge, at sunset

  • Hours: 8:30am–7:45pm daily

Day 3
Sirolo (Conero Riviera)

"Where the mountain meets the sea, and the mussels are hand-picked by divers."
Baia di Portonovo, seen from Monte Conero

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈95 km from Fano  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈1h30, more with stops

15 September 2026 is a Tuesday. Gelateria del Conero closed Mondays (not relevant); Diecidodici restaurant closed Mondays (not relevant); Abbazia di Portonovo only opens Thursday/Saturday 5–7pm (relevant, see note).

Routing note: Senigallia sits directly on the coastal route south from Fano, ~30km down — worth a real stop. The Rocca Roveresca (15th-century fortress, €5 entry) and historic centre justify 45–60 minutes. Continue south to Portonovo before Sirolo.

Ortona & San Vito Chietino

"Dinner suspended over the sea on a working fishing machine."
A trabocco, Marina di San Vito Chietino

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈210 km from Sirolo, including the Ortona stop  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈3h15

16 September 2026 is a Wednesday. Genuinely the emptiest, longest driving day on the route.

Routing note — Loreto (optional): The Basilica della Santa Casa is barely off the direct route, one of the most significant Marian pilgrimage sites in Christianity. Free entry, 6:30am–7:30pm daily. Ortona — a working port town
📍 42.3535835, 14.4032270 — Corso Vittorio Emanuele, Ortona CH
Castello Aragonese, Ortona
  • Why stop: A genuine port town. The Cattedrale di San Tommaso holds the relics of the Apostle Thomas, stolen from Chios by an Ortonese sailor named Leone in 1258, along with the original tombstone bearing a Greek inscription. The annual Feast of the Pardon celebrates this with a Renaissance-style procession; add Castello Aragonese and a wander through the old town around it.
  • Time required: 1–1.5 hours

Contrada San Donato, about 5km south of Ortona  ·  Free entry  ·  Always open
  • Why stop: WWII Commonwealth war cemetery from the December 1943 Battle of Ortona, with 1,615 graves, including 1,375 Canadian graves in the official CWGC count. A quiet way to understand why this small port has such weight in Canadian military memory.
  • Time required: 20–30 min

📍 42.2307442, 14.3903892 — Piazza del Plebiscito, 66034 Lanciano CH  ·  ≈11 km, 15–20 min by car; direct 13-min train from San Vito–Lanciano station
Piazza del Plebiscito, Lanciano

A genuine historic town, not a beach-resort day trip. The old centre is split into four medieval quarters — Lancianovecchia, Civitanova, Sacca, Borgo — each with its own coat of arms and flag, built across three hills.

Food detour: Bottega del Bocconotto in Castel Frentano, about 5km from Lanciano, was founded in 2008 by Claudia and Pierino Bucci using a recipe from grandmother "Sabbiuccia." Overview

19 September 2026 is a Saturday. This is the turn into the Gargano: limestone beaches, white-stone lanes, a working port below the old town, and one of the route's stranger pairings of history and pastry.

Do not force the Lesina detour if time is tight. It is genuinely interesting, but it adds real driving time for a lagoon-and-eels experience that is a specific taste, not a broad crowd-pleaser.

20 September 2026 is a Sunday. The Baia delle Zagare stop adds a there-and-back detour off the SP53, so this is either a short scenic transfer or a half-day beach-access mission.

Routing note: The SP53 coastal road between Vieste and Mattinata is itself the scenic experience: cliffs dropping straight into the Adriatic most of the way.
Panoramic view of Mattinata

Stops

Baia delle Zagare
📍 41.7311, 16.0857 — Comune di Mattinata, FG; cove has no street address
Faraglione, Baia delle Zagare
  • Why stop: Two limestone faraglioni rising from turquoise water between white cliffs: one of the Gargano's most photographed spots, and genuinely earns it.
  • Time required: Half day if you get in; the access process alone can eat an hour
  • Access: Free public beach access is capped at 20 passes/day in high season, issued each morning at the Info Point, Corso Matino 64, Mattinata. Arrive early, bring ID, and expect all adults in the party to be present.
  • Alternatives: Paid cliff elevator, day-use sunbed hire at Baia dei Mergoli lido, or access through the on-site hotel.
Reported costs online vary wildly, from roughly €15 for access up to sunbed/parking fees near €70+ combined. 20 September is past deepest high season, so call Info Point (+39 0884 597291) before assuming August-level restrictions.
Practical note: The bay falls into shade by mid-afternoon due to the cliffs. Go in the morning if the sun matters.

Coffee & Pasticceria

Pasticceria La Torre Via Giacomo Matteotti
📍 41.7110463, 16.0511548  ·  📞 +39 366 150 2382  ·  4.8★/152 reviews

Run by Francesco and his son Davide. Reviewers repeatedly describe Francesco as "a character" who gives an informal tasting before you choose your flavour. Several ice cream and pastry creations are invented in-house, including almond sticks used as edible spoons.

Genuinely warm, family-run, not a polished operation: exactly the kind of place this route is after.

Dinner

Giardino di Monsignore Contrada Torre del Porto

Beach-club restaurant right on the sand, around €48/person with wine. Pricier than average, but reviews back it up.

Locanda del Maniscalco Via Luigi Zuppetta 12/14

Alternate dinner option: genuine farm-to-table, run by the De Vita family.

Dal Saraceno Via Vincenzo Amicarelli 31a

Budget pick in the Junno quarter: family-run since 1967, a historic pizzeria on a characterful staircase street, honest prices, widely called the best pizza in town.

Local Speciality

Extra virgin olive oil — Mattinata sits in genuine olive country, and it shows in the food; look for it drizzled generously rather than sparingly. Also look for orecchiette, caciocavallo podolico, and whatever Giardino pulls from the sea that day.

A genuine family-run oil mill using cold-pressed native Gargano varieties: Ogliarola Garganica, Coratina, Peranzana, and Frantoio. Reviewers specifically call out the honesty and professionalism of the operation.

Hidden Gem

Oleificio Le Monache doubles as both What to Buy and Hidden Gem here: an ancient family tradition, per their own description, still guiding daily decisions. If the Sunday closure rules it out for this trip, it is worth remembering as a reason to build in a weekday return to Mattinata on a future visit.

Cool, Interesting & Quirky

Two genuine guided experiences run through the Mattinata tourism office on Corso Matino 64 (+39 0884 597291): a sunrise hike to Monte Saraceno, departing 6:00am, roughly 2 hours; and the "Love Trail" from Vignanotica to Mergoli, departing 5:30pm, roughly 3 hours.

Abbazia della Santissima Trinità di Monte Sacro Monte Sacro, 874m

The trail is steep and unmarked, not a casual stroll.

Day 9
Monte Sant'Angelo & Trani

"A cave where an archangel is said to have stood, then a cathedral floating on the sea."
Cattedrale di Trani

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈95 km Mattinata to Monte Sant'Angelo to Trani, plus ≈45 km to Molfetta or ≈50 km to Bari  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈3.5-4 hours total including both main stops

21 September 2026 is a Monday. This is the longest, most decision-heavy day on the route: Monte Sant'Angelo works as a lunch-only half-day stop, Trani gets the afternoon, and the evening comes down to a real choice between sleeping in Molfetta or Bari.

Routing note: This is not a dawdle day. Give Monte Sant'Angelo enough time for the sanctuary, keep Trani's cathedral and harbour walk protected, then decide before leaving Trani whether tonight should be quiet or lively.
Monte Sant'Angelo street and sanctuary tower

Stops

Monte Sant'Angelo — Santuario di San Michele Arcangelo
📍 41.7080134, 15.9547699 — Via Reale Basilica 127, 71037 Monte Sant'Angelo FG  ·  📞 +39 0884 561150
Santuario di San Michele Arcangelo
  • Why stop: A UNESCO-listed sanctuary built around the cave where the Archangel Michael is said to have appeared in the 5th century; one of Western Christianity's oldest continuously visited pilgrimage sites.
  • Time required: 1.5-2 hours
  • Worth the detour: Yes. Even without the religious layer, the medieval hilltop town and Gargano views justify the stop.
  • Approx cost: Free
  • Hours: 7:30am-7:00pm daily
Monte Sant'Angelo — Rione Junno & Rione Grotte
Historic quarters below the Sanctuary
  • Why stop: Two distinct historic quarters below the Sanctuary. Rione Junno is the old town's whitewashed, single-room terraced houses, narrow stairs, and lanes built around the pilgrim economy. Rione Grotte is the adjacent poorer quarter where residents lived in cave dwellings actually carved into the rock.
  • Do not conflate them: Junno is built stone; Grotte is rock-cut.
  • Time required: 30–45 min
Trani — the Cathedral on the Sea
📍 41.2821965, 16.4183605 — Piazza Duomo 1, 76125 Trani BT  ·  📞 +39 0883 500293
  • Why stop: A Romanesque cathedral built directly on the water's edge in local pinkish-white stone. The point is the serenity and restraint, not ornament overload.
  • Time required: 1.5-2 hours for the cathedral, castle, old town, and port
  • Worth the detour: Yes
  • Optional climb: Tower climb is about €5 and roughly 298 steps, with views over the castle and harbour.
Sinagoga Scolanova Giudecca quarter, Trani
  • Why stop: Built in the 13th century, converted to a church after the forced conversions of the 1290s, and returned to Jewish worship in 2004 for the first time in roughly 700 years. Now run by the Naples Jewish community.
  • Visual note: The bell tower and Star of David on the same building make it a stronger historical counterpoint to the cathedral-on-the-sea framing than another waterfront stop.
  • Time required: 20–30 min
  • Approx cost/hours: Free entry Monday/Wednesday/Friday/Sunday at 11:00am; guided tours by reservation

Coffee & Pasticceria

Pasticceria Ciuffreda Monte Sant'Angelo
📍 41.7074364, 15.9541869 — Via Giuseppe Garibaldi 15  ·  📞 +39 349 730 5968  ·  4.8★/39 reviews

Running since 1975. Stick to the local specialties: poperati and cartellate are Monte Sant'Angelo's own, and the most useful review notes owner AnnaPina explaining and customising a poperato-style ricotta-and-chocolate dessert on the spot.

Honest flag: one review calls out almond biscuits with an unpleasant chemical taste. Rare miss, but enough reason to favour fresh pasticciotti and cartellate over packaged items.
Bar Europa Trani
📍 41.2766535, 16.4174057 — Corso Vittorio Emanuele 161  ·  📞 +39 0883 583483  ·  4.7★/669 reviews

Open late, founded by the Farucci family in 1966, and recognised by Regione Puglia as a Locale Storico. The signature is Tette delle Monache: delicate, oversized pastries that reviewers repeatedly call out as good value.

Lunch

Ristorante Medioevo Best pick

Via Castello 21, near the castle. Historic setting, 4.5★ across 745+ reviews, and built around Gargano ingredients without sliding into tasting-menu formality.

Al Barone Alternate

Rustic-elegant option in the centro storico near the Basilica, roughly €25/person.

Ristorante San Michele Budget

Via Reale Basilica 51, right next to the Basilica, around €10-20/person.

Dinner & Overnight

Option A: Molfetta
If you stay in Trani instead, Pescaria has expanded from its 2015 Polignano a Mare origin into a 10+ location national chain, so it is no longer a small local find, though the company says the seafood is still sourced daily from Puglia.

Quieter, smaller, more purely local: the honest choice if tonight should feel like a rest rather than another city to absorb.

Dinner: Dentro Le Mura, Corso Dante Alighieri 42, is the most reliably verified pick in Molfetta itself. Budget option: La Cucina del Mare, a counter-style gastronomia on Via Alfredo Baccarini.

Accommodation: Il Mulino di Amleto, Vicolo Campanile 6, a literary-themed B&B with a rooftop terrace and a host, Antonella, repeatedly praised by name.

No genuine $$$ splurge has turned up in Molfetta itself; the town's dining tops out around €25-30/person. Also, Bue Marino is excellent but in Bisceglie, not walkable from a Molfetta overnight.
Option B: Bari

Bigger, louder, and livelier, with Bari Vecchia to explore in the evening or the following morning.

Dinner: Al Pescatore, Piazza Federico II di Svevia 6/8, is the special-occasion seafood institution by Castello Svevo. Alternate: Al Sorso Preferito / Urban Assassineria for the Spaghetti all'Assassina story. Budget: Panificio Fiore for focaccia barese.

Accommodation: Bari Vecchia Dimora, Corte Lamberti 6, puts you right in the old town. Request a lower floor if stairs are a concern.

Spaghetti all'assassina, Bari

Local Speciality

Molfetta: Adriatic seafood: brodetto and whatever the day's catch was. No distinctly Molfetta-specific speciality has turned up in the research, which is a real gap rather than something to paper over.

Bari: Spaghetti all'assassina: pasta deliberately cooked in a hot pan until part-charred, a genuine Bari invention with a disputed-origin story attached.

What to Buy

On Strada Arco Basso, the better purchase may be attention rather than pasta: stop for a genuine 10 minutes and watch the speed and handwork before deciding whether you want a souvenir bag.

Hidden Gem

Basilica di San Nicola in Bari Vecchia is worth knowing about if the Bari route wins: an 11th-century basilica holding relics of the real St Nicholas, with significance across both Catholic and Orthodox Christianity.

Cool, Interesting & Quirky

Strada Arco Basso, Bari Vecchia
📍 41.1273126, 16.8671134 — Arco Basso, 70122 Bari BA

Bari Vecchia's famous orecchiette street: women working at floured tables outside their doors, shaping pasta with a quick thumb motion while trays dry overhead. The skill on display is real and generational.

Honest flag from a local source: some reporting alleges that part of what's sold here is wholesale pasta from factories in the Bari province, resold at artisanal prices. Watch the craft, enjoy the theatre, support the street if you want, but don't assume every bag was made by the exact hands in front of you.

If Time Is Tight

Skip Monte Sant'Angelo's town wander and stick to the Basilica itself. In Trani, the cathedral exterior and a walk along the port cover the essentials.

If You've Got Half a Day

Climb Trani's cathedral tower, then set aside 20-30 minutes to actually watch, not just photograph, the orecchiette-making on Strada Arco Basso if Bari is in play.

Skip It

Don't buy orecchiette on Strada Arco Basso specifically expecting small-batch artisanal sourcing. Buy it as a souvenir and a way of supporting the street's continued life, not as a provenance guarantee.

🍝 Lunch: Ristorante Medioevo, Monte Sant'Angelo
✨ Experience: Santuario di San Michele Arcangelo
🛍️ Purchase: A photograph and 10 real minutes on Strada Arco Basso

Day 10
Polignano a Mare, Monopoli & San Vito dei Normanni

"The last coastal day: cove, fish-charcuterie lab, quiet old port, white city, rock-carved aperitivo."
Lama Monachile, Polignano a Mare

Overview

DRIVING DISTANCE: ≈55-70 km depending on Molfetta/Bari start  ·  DRIVING TIME: ≈2-2.5 hours actual driving

22 September 2026 is a Tuesday. This is a full stop-and-go coastal day: Polignano first, Monopoli for the old port and lunch, Ostuni for the late-afternoon White City, then San Vito dei Normanni for the arrival-night base.

Routing note: Polignano comes before Monopoli because it sits further north. That avoids backtracking and gets you to Lama Monachile before the cove gets fully busy.
  • 8:30am: Depart Bari/Molfetta
  • 9:05-10:45am: Lama Monachile, Polignano old town, and caffè speciale
  • ~11:05am: Dorsale fish-charcuterie tasting
  • 11:50am-1:45pm: Monopoli centro storico and lunch
  • 2:25-5:30pm: Ostuni old town, Forno 31, and viewpoint
  • 6:00-6:45pm: Riccardo Caffè aperitivo
  • 7:00pm: Arrive San Vito dei Normanni; 7:30pm dinner at XFood if confirmed
Riccardo Caffè does not open until 6:00pm. Sources disagree on whether XFood is open Tuesdays, so call ahead and book rather than relying on showing up.

Stops

Polignano a Mare — Lama Monachile
📍 40.9963887, 17.2181307 — Via S. Vito, 70044 Polignano a Mare BA  ·  PARKING: can be busy; white-marked areas only
  • Why stop: The postcard cove between two cliffs, with houses stacked to the water's edge above it. It is genuinely striking and genuinely crowded.
  • Time required: 1-2 hours
  • Worth the detour: Worth changing today's plans for
  • Practical note: Not a sandy beach. Bring water shoes for the large pebbles.
Monopoli — Centro Storico
📍 40.9508368, 17.3033327 — Largo Cattedrale, 70043 Monopoli BA  ·  📞 +39 080 742253
Old port, Monopoli
  • Why stop: Less touristed than Polignano or Bari, but just as handsome: Cattedrale della Madia, Castello Carlo V, and a working old port with fishing boats.
  • Time required: 1.5-2 hours
  • Worth the detour: Yes
  • Parking: Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II is the closest paid option; free white-line spots are further out at Piazza XX Settembre or Via Procaccia.
  • Hours (Castello): 10:00am-2:00pm and 4:00-8:00pm daily
Rifugi Antiaerei di Monopoli
Under Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II, Monopoli
  • Why stop: WWII air-raid shelters under the main square, around 300m of tunnels dug into tufo rock, once holding up to 1,800 people during bombing raids. Preserved wartime graffiti and infirmary rooms make it a sharper historical layer under the otherwise sunny old town.
  • Time required: 45–60 min with guided tour
  • Approx cost/hours: Comune-run guided tours, summer weekend hours 18:00–21:00, €3 ticket; book via the Piazza Garibaldi Info Point
Ostuni — Centro Storico ("La Terra")
📍 40.7333725, 17.5778841 — Centro Storico, 72017 Ostuni BR  ·  PARKING: ZTL inside old centre; park outside the walls
Ostuni, the White City
  • Why stop: Whitewashed houses, narrow stepped alleys inside Aragonese walls, and the Gothic Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta with its rose window.
  • Time required: Afternoon through early evening, including Riccardo Caffè
  • Best time: Late afternoon into early evening; Piazzetta Martiri delle Foibe gives the classic town-on-the-hill view.
  • Honest note: In peak season the centre gets busy by early afternoon. The cathedral-to-walls backstreets can still be genuinely quiet.

Coffee & Pasticceria

Caffè speciale Polignano a Mare

The real local curiosity is not a café by name but the drink itself: espresso whipped with sugar, lemon peel, cream, and amaretto liqueur. Ask for it by name around the old town and Lama Monachile.

Forno 31 Ostuni
Via G. Ferrari 6, 72017 Ostuni BR
Pasticciotti with custard and sour cherry

A working bakery built around an original 15th-century wood-fired oven, using 2-metre-long paddles. Go for pasticciotti in multiple fillings, plus focaccia, taralli, and friselle.

Lunch & Dinner

Madià Lunch, Monopoli

At the Porto Antico: panzerotto eaten on a bench by the old port watching fishing boats. This is a local street-food ritual, and it suits the day's pacing better than a long sit-down lunch.

Panzerotti pugliesi
Locanda dei Mercanti Alternate

Reportedly the best pasticciotto in Monopoli, served as a tris of three fillings, with savoury lunch if you want to sit down properly.

XFood Dinner, San Vito dei Normanni
Via Mare snc, inside ExFadda cultural centre, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR  ·  📞 +39 366 891 3041

Puglia's first social restaurant: a ten-year-running project staffed partly by people with disabilities, serving well-regarded Pugliese cooking, with €27-70pp depending on menu.

Honest flag: sources conflict on Tuesday opening hours. Book ahead by phone because tonight is a Tuesday.

Local Speciality

Panzerotto: Puglia's fried, folded street food, best today in Monopoli.

Pasticciotto: best today at Forno 31 in Ostuni.

Caffè speciale: Polignano's own coffee-and-amaretto ritual, unique to the town.

What to Buy

Taralli or friselle from Forno 31. Unlike the pasticciotto, these travel well and keep for weeks, so they are a genuine take-home option rather than an eat-immediately one.

Hidden Gems

Dorsale — fish charcuterie lab, Polignano a Mare

A fish butcher that cures seafood the way a salumeria cures pork: squid-and-tuna mortadella, salmon-pistachio prosciutto, swordfish ham, tuna guanciale. Opened December 2025, the first of its kind. Treat it as a curiosity tasting between Lama Monachile and departure, not a lunch substitute.

Riccardo Caffè — rock-carved bar, Ostuni
📍 Via Gaetano Tanzarella Vitale 61, 72017 Ostuni BR  ·  Opens 6:00pm daily

A former 13th-century flour mill turned evening cocktail/aperitivo bar carved directly into white limestone: stalactites, stone rooms, and a stepped stone entrance with a view over the White City.

Virgola Pasticceria Terapeutica — San Vito dei Normanni
📍 Via Mesagne 136, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR

A tomorrow-morning flag rather than tonight's stop: a therapeutic pastry lab run by Nicola Di Lena, training women who are domestic-violence survivors and young people on the autism spectrum. Same social project as XFood.

Cool, Interesting & Quirky

Monopoli sits on a hidden network of rock-cut Byzantine churches carved into the ravines outside town, including Cripta dello Spirito Santo and Chiesa dei Santi Andrea e Procopio.

Honest flag: these are obscure even by Puglia standards. One opens only eight days a year for Easter tradition; others sit on private masseria land and need a guide arranged in advance. Interesting for a future slower visit, not realistic today.

Arrival: San Vito dei Normanni

📍 40.6563995, 17.7087266 — Via Principe di Piemonte 13, 72019 San Vito dei Normanni BR

The final hop from Ostuni is about 15-20 minutes. This page only covers arrival; San Vito gets the full local treatment in the separate Puglia-basing document. For tonight: arrive, settle in, dinner at XFood.

If Time Is Tight

Skip Monopoli entirely. Polignano with Dorsale, then Ostuni with Riccardo Caffè, then San Vito dei Normanni is a complete, satisfying day on its own.

If You've Got Half a Day

Add the full Monopoli old town and castle walk, and take the extra time at Forno 31 and Ostuni's viewpoint rather than rushing toward Riccardo Caffè.

Skip It

Do not chase the rupestrian churches today. They are not accessible without advance arrangement, and trying anyway will eat time better spent at Riccardo Caffè.
